Mating Behaviors as Buffers for Climate Impacts in Sea Turtle Populations

Location: Brazil (nesting beaches) • Status: Ongoing • Collaborators: Dr. X, Dr. Y

Overview: Investigating how warming nesting temperatures affect adult sex ratios and whether maternal strategies buffer offspring thermal tolerance.

Show project details

Where it works: Field sites on the northeast Brazilian coast; lab analyses at UMass and partner labs.

Collaborators: Local conservation groups, nesting beach field teams, genetics lab at UMass.

Field work: Nest monitoring, temperature loggers, sample collection for hatchlings and maternal tissues.

Lab work: Incubation experiments, sexing of hatchlings, physiological assays.

Bioinformatics: Genotyping, kinship analysis, population demographic modeling.

What was fun: Long nights on the beach watching hatchlings emerge and cross-checking field notes with local collaborators.

Cold tolerance mechanisms in range-expanding black sea bass populations

Location: Northeastern US coast • Status: Ongoing • Collaborators: Dr. A, Dr. B

Overview: Studying whether northern, range-expanding black sea bass populations rely on genetic adaptation or plasticity to tolerate extreme cold events.

Show project details

Where it works: Field collections along the northern range edge and common-garden experiments at partner labs.

Collaborators: Fisheries labs, NOAA partners, student researchers.

Field work: Seasonal sampling, tagging, and in situ temperature monitoring.

Lab work: Thermal tolerance assays, metabolic rate measurements, histology.

Bioinformatics: Population genomics, selection scans, transcriptomic responses to cold shock.

What was fun: Bringing together physiology and genomics — and the thrill of a successful cold-challenge run.
