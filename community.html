Community & Values

I strive to create research and learning spaces where people can do their best work as their authentic selves. I value clear communication, kindness, and inclusive collaboration—because better science comes from broader participation and respectful teams. In my work, I aim to remove barriers to participation through mentoring, outreach, and service activities that support diversity, equity, inclusion, and justice.

What I Do

  • Teaching: Developing course content and facilitating learning in physiology, genomics, and field ecology. I prioritize active learning and accessibility for all students.
  • Outreach: Engaging K-12 students and communities in science through workshops, public talks, and experiential learning events.
  • Service: Contributing to departmental and disciplinary initiatives; serving on committees focused on student welfare and inclusion.
  • DEIJ Initiatives: Supporting diversity, equity, inclusion, and justice efforts in academia and STEM. I mentor students from underrepresented backgrounds and work to make science accessible.

Mentoring Approach

I believe good mentoring means meeting people where they are and helping them grow in directions that matter to them—whether that's research, professional development, or personal resilience in academia.

What students can expect from working with me:

  • Clear expectations: We'll set goals together and discuss timelines, workload, and how I can best support your learning.
  • Regular check-ins: I schedule routine meetings to discuss progress, troubleshoot challenges, and celebrate wins.
  • Feedback on your work: I provide constructive, timely feedback on lab work, writing, presentations, and analyses.
  • Professional development: I support training in communication, data analysis, grant writing, and navigating academia.
  • Authentic support: I'm interested in you as a whole person—your career goals, challenges, and well-being matter.
  • Honest conversations: If something isn't working, we'll talk about it directly and figure out next steps together.

Resources & Opportunities

How to get involved:

Students (undergraduate or graduate):

  • Interested in research on thermal biology or climate adaptation? Reach out with your interests and background.
  • Looking to join lab meetings or learn about field work? I welcome observers and collaborators at all levels.
  • Seeking mentorship in science, career planning, or navigating academia? Let's set up a conversation.

Collaborators & community partners:

  • I'm interested in field partnerships, especially for sea turtle and marine fish research.
  • Interdisciplinary collaborations welcome—particularly bridging physiology, genomics, ecology, and conservation.
  • Community science and citizen science initiatives aligned with my research.

Public & K-12 engagement:

  • I give talks on climate adaptation, sea turtles, and thermal biology to schools and community groups.
  • Interested in developing curriculum or organizing a science workshop? Let's collaborate.
  • See my CV for outreach activities and past events.
